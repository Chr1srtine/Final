Christine Park is a fourth-year violin performance major studying under Professor Kenneth Renshaw at the College-Conservatory of Music (CCM). After returning to the United States with her family and relocating to Raleigh in 2017, she remained actively involved in musical activities. She served as concertmaster of her middle and high school orchestras and performed with the Triangle Youth Symphony and the Triangle Youth Philharmonic. She was also selected to participate in the NC Eastern Regional Orchestra, the North Carolina All-State Orchestra, and the NC Honors Orchestra. In 2018, Christine performed at the NC Bach Festival Benefit Gala Concert and appeared at Carnegie Hall as part of a summer program organized by the Honors Performance Series. In 2020, she was selected to attend the North Carolina Governor’s School West. After years of volunteering in music therapy and assisting with teaching, she decided to fully dedicate herself to music and began her studies at UNCSA under Professor Kevin Lawrence in the fall of 2020. Christine has participated in numerous summer music festivals, including Green Mountain Music Festival, Ascent Music Festival, and Miami Summer Music Festival. She has been awarded full scholarships to programs such as the Chicago Summer Opera Festival and the Miami Summer Music Festival. In addition, she has had the honor of receiving chamber music masterclasses from Masumi Rostad and the Miro String Quartet.
